Game Phases

Understanding the game flow is crucial to winning. Each phase has specific actions and time limits.

üé≤ Setup Phase (30 seconds)

What happens:

  • Game begins with player role distribution based on preset
  • Roles are randomly shuffled and assigned to players
  • Players receive a DM with their role card and abilities
  • Game state is initialized

Player Actions:

  • Read your role card carefully
  • Understand your abilities and win condition
  • Note if you have night actions or special abilities
  • Prepare your strategy
üåô Night Phase (60 seconds)

What happens:

  • All players are muted in voice chat
  • Players with night abilities receive action prompts via DM
  • Wasps can communicate in their team chat
  • Medium Bee can communicate with dead players
  • Actions are processed simultaneously at the end of the night phase

Player Actions:

  • Check your DMs for action prompts
  • Select targets using buttons if you have a night action
  • Wasps vote on who to kill
  • Wait for night results

Examples of Night Actions:

  • Investigators learn information about players
  • Killers choose targets
  • Protectors choose who to guard or heal
  • Roleblockers choose who to block
  • Watchers/Trackers observe player movements
üåÜ Dusk Phase (60 seconds, optional)

What happens:

  • Occurs after night, before day
  • ONLY for roles with dusk actions
  • Auto-skips if no players have dusk actions
  • Auto-skips remaining players after timeout

Roles with Dusk Actions:

  • Jailer Bee: Selects who to jail tonight and decides whether to execute
  • Transporter Bee: Selects two players to swap
  • Pirate Beetle: Selects duel target and rock/paper/scissors choice

Special Rules:

  • Debug mode: 30 seconds instead of 60
  • Bot players auto-complete after 2 seconds
  • If phase is skipped, players proceed to day immediately
‚òÄÔ∏è Day Phase (180 seconds)

What happens:

  • Muting is lifted for alive players (except special cases)
  • Night results are announced
  • Players can discuss and share information
  • Players can claim roles or accuse others

Voice Chat Rules:

  • Dead players: Remain muted
  • Blackmailed players: Voice muted, text transformed
  • Deceived players: Voice muted, text twisted to sound incriminating
  • Mute Bee variants: Always muted, messages converted to emojis
  • Deaf Bees: Always deafened (cannot hear voice)

Strategy Tips:

  • Share investigation results (if you're an investigator)
  • Listen carefully to what others say
  • Look for contradictions in claims
  • Watch for suspicious behavior
  • Coordinate with your team

Special Day Actions:

  • Queen Bee: Can reveal for 3 extra votes (4 total)
  • Marshal Bee: Can choose one player to protect from lynching
üó≥Ô∏è Voting Phase (120 seconds)

What happens:

  • Players vote for who to eliminate
  • Votes are displayed in real-time
  • Players can change their vote before time expires
  • Vote tally is calculated at the end

Voting Mechanics:

  • Each player gets 1 vote
  • Queen Bee (revealed) gets 4 votes
  • Minimum 2 votes needed to eliminate (1 in debug mode)
  • Players can vote "Skip" to abstain

Tied Vote Resolution:

  1. First tie detected ‚Üí Revote triggered
  2. All votes cleared, 60-second revote begins
  3. Only tied players can be voted on
  4. If still tied after revote ‚Üí Random player from tied group is eliminated
  5. Results announced in text channel

Special Voting Mechanics:

  • Judge: Can force a revote once per game to change outcome
  • Marshal Bee: If protected Bee is voted out:
    • Marshal reveals themselves
    • Save succeeds, Bee survives
    • Fails if target is Wasp/Evil Neutral
  • Phantom Moth: First lynch doesn't kill them, becomes invisible for 1 cycle

Post-Elimination:

  • Eliminated player dies and becomes "dead player"
  • Dead players muted in voice chat
  • Dead players can communicate with Medium Bee and other dead
  • Clown Beetle (Jester): Can haunt one guilty voter after being lynched
üèÜ Win Check

After each voting phase, the game checks for win conditions:

  • Bee Team Wins: All Wasps are dead AND all harmful Neutrals are dead
  • Wasp Team Wins: Wasps ‚â• (Bees + harmful Neutrals)
  • Cult Wins: All alive players are Cultists or converted
  • Neutral Killer Solo Win: Only one player remains and they're Neutral Killing
  • Specific Neutral Wins: Various conditions depending on role

If no win condition is met:

  • Game continues to next night phase
  • Cycle repeats until a team wins

Tips for Each Phase

üåô Night Phase Tips

  • Always submit your action before time runs out
  • Wasps: Coordinate who to kill in team chat
  • Investigators: Target suspicious players
  • Protectors: Protect confirmed town members or yourself
  • Don't forget you have a night action!

‚òÄÔ∏è Day Phase Tips

  • Share your investigation results (but be careful not to reveal too much)
  • Pay attention to who is silent or defensive
  • Watch for contradictions in role claims
  • Build alliances with confirmed Bees
  • Wasps: Blend in and spread doubt

üó≥Ô∏è Voting Phase Tips

  • Vote based on evidence, not just suspicion
  • Watch the vote count - don't accidentally vote a Bee
  • Coordinate with other Bees to secure majority
  • Remember you can change your vote before time expires
  • Wasps: Try to control the vote or blend in

üåÜ Dusk Phase Tips

  • Jailer: Jail suspicious players or confirmed threats
  • Transporter: Swap to protect key players or disrupt Wasp kills
  • Pirate: Choose your RPS move wisely based on psychology
  • Submit quickly - phase auto-skips after all actions are in
